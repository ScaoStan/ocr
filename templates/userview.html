ABOUT GRADING SYSTEMS

Grades can be given as letters (often A through F), a range (such as 1 to 6), a percentage, or a number as a percentage of the total possible. The grades associated with marks awarded for each course taken are listed in the academic transcript. There are different types of grading systems which include:

i. Percentage Grading: The percentage scale, which is expressed as a percentage of the scores, is being used. Typically, it is determined through tests and/or the percentage of points obtained on assignments. Percentage based trascripts usually include the percentage scores in the transcripts

ii. Letter Grading:It is achieved by using an ordered category scale using a sequence of letters (often A, B, C, D, and F) or letters with plusses and minuses using a standard-based approach. Letter based transcripts usually include the Letter grade in the transcripts. Often times,they do not contain the percentage scores as more emphasis are placed on the letter grades

iii. Mastery Grading:When a student's proficiency reaches a certain level, they are graded as "masters" or "passers," with various pupils often being given varying periods of time to achieve mastery.

iv. Pass or Fail:Using a pass/fail scale, occasionally in conjunction with mastery grading. For example, the United Kingdom uses the Pass/Fail grading system.

iv. Narrative GradingMaking remarks on students' accomplishments in instead of or in addition to using numbers or letters.
